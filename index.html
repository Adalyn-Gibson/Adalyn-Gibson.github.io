About Me

My journey as a fourth-year student in the astrophysical and planetary sciences department at the University of Colorado Boulder has been enriched by my active involvement in undergraduate research. Since my first year, I have been part of research initiatives studying debris disks and exocomets. Since the Spring semester of 2024, I have expanded my research horizons to focus on studying stellar flares. In the Spring of 2025, I successfully defended my Latin Honors thesis, "Probing Non-Thermal Processes in Stellar Flares on AU Mic." This hands-on experience has deepened my understanding of the subject and honed my research skills. Complementing my research, I am deeply committed to science education and have actively contributed to science education outreach at Fiske Planetarium. My passion for science education has also led me to be a Learning Assistant, where I assisted in the instruction of the ASTR 2600 - Introduction to Scientific Programming course.
